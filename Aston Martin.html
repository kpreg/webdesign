Aston Martin

Aston Martin's brand heritage includes some of the most prestigious racecars ever made. Aston's cultural status as the gentleman's car has given Aston a strong base of loyal customers to continue to buy their cars, through thick or thin. Their tireless dedication to upholding their sophisticated brand image has resulted in a model portfolio of incredibly beautiful, detailed works of art.


In my opinion, Aston's best car is their upcoming DBS Superlegerra. For the uninitiated, Aston has several teirs of cars spanning across two classes: the sports cars, and the grand touring cars. Their sports cars are playful, fun, lightweight, and handle well. Their GT cars, while still sporty and fun to drive, are precision machines with high horsepower and the unique ability to withstand ludicrously high speeds over long periods of time.

The DBS Superleggera

A side profile of a grey Superlegerra


The DBS Superlegerra is Aston Martin’s most powerful GT car ever made. A direct response to Ferrari’s introduction of the 812 Superfast, this front-engined V12 monster is the most powerful road-going Aston Martin ever made. No compromises are made on this car, from the opulent interior to the 911-like handling. Every stitch, every bolt, and every fastener is meticulously assembled by a team of well trained professionals at the Aston Martin factory in the UK.

The DBS Superleggera is a variant of Aston Martin’s grand touring car, the DB11. While still a fantastic car, Aston has improved immensely upon the original design of the DB11, sparing no expense in order to make it faster, stiffer, and better handling than its competition. Modifications include more extensive carbon fiber use throughout the chassis for weight reduction, a tune to the already powerful twin-turbo V12 to allow for 715HP, and more aggressive aerodynamic features throughout. The result is a truly class-splitting car of GT origin with characteristics rivaling the mid-engined competition from Ferrari and Lamborghini.
